# Damaged Shipment

Use this skill when a customer says an order arrived damaged, defective, or
unsafe to use.

Workflow:

1. Acknowledge the damage clearly and apologize for the arrival condition.
2. Confirm whether the issue was present on arrival or appeared after use.
3. Ask for the order number and, when relevant, one or two photos of the item
   and packaging for operations review.
4. Offer the fastest supported resolution path: replacement first for damaged
   deliveries, refund when the customer prefers not to receive another unit.
5. If there is a safety concern such as battery swelling, sparks, broken glass,
   or leaking fluid, instruct the customer to stop using the item immediately
   and escalate the case the same day.
6. Damage-on-arrival cases may still qualify for help even when the normal
   return window is close or has just passed.

When the request is specifically about damage in transit or a defective arrival,
lead with the replacement-or-refund workflow instead of general return policy
language.
